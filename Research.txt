URBAN HEAT ISLAND RESEARCH ARTICLE

Title: "Urban heat: an increasing threat to global health"
Source: BMJ (British Medical Journal) - PMC
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8543181/

Key Findings:

1. OVERVIEW OF URBAN HEAT ISLAND EFFECT
- Urban areas are generally warmer than adjacent rural and suburban areas
- For example, London's city center can be 4°C higher than outer rural areas
- During extreme heat events, this differential can reach 10°C, with the largest differential at night
- Caused by materials like metal, concrete, and brick that efficiently absorb and store heat from the sun

2. HEALTH IMPACTS
- Children born in 2020 will experience 2-7 times more extreme heat events compared to those born in 1960
- Urban heat stress poses major risk to public health
- Disproportionately affects marginalized and low-income communities

3. GLOBAL SIGNIFICANCE
- World's urban population expected to grow from 4.2 billion (2018) to 6.7 billion by 2050
- Climate change, urbanization, and aging population converge to increase heat-related risks
- Extreme heat events increasing worldwide due to human-induced climate change

4. MITIGATION STRATEGIES
According to research on urban heat islands:
- Energy efficiency improvements
- Urban landscape optimization
- Green roof construction
- High reflectivity material utilization
- Green land cultivation
- Remote sensing technology and numerical simulation methods for research

This peer-reviewed article provides comprehensive analysis of urban heat island effects and their impact on public health, making it an excellent resource for understanding this critical environmental phenomenon.
